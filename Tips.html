Sri Lankan Travel Guidelines

Religious Practices

Temple Dress Code

  • Wear modest clothing that covers shoulders and knees.
  • Remove shoes before entering temples.
  • Avoid wearing hats inside sacred places.
  • Refrain from wearing flashy or tight clothing.
  • Use a shawl or scarf if needed for covering.
  • Respect the local dress traditions.
  • Ensure your attire is clean and neat.
  • Women can carry a sarong for easy adjustments.

Temple Behavior

  • Maintain silence inside the temple premises.
  • Do not turn your back to Buddha statues.
  • Ask permission before taking photos.
  • Avoid touching sacred objects or paintings.
  • Walk clockwise around the stupa.
  • Do not point your feet towards sacred objects.
  • Refrain from smoking or eating in the temple area.
  • Follow the instructions of temple staff or monks.

Wildlife Safety

Elephant Encounters

  • Maintain a safe distance from wild elephants.
  • Never attempt to feed or approach them.
  • Avoid driving close to elephants on rural roads.
  • Do not use flash photography around elephants.
  • Be cautious during mating or calving seasons.
  • Respect elephant crossing signs on highways.
  • Stay alert in elephant-prone areas, especially at night.
  • Report aggressive elephant encounters to authorities.

Safari Guidelines

  • Stay in vehicles during safaris.
  • Follow the guide's instructions at all times.
  • Refrain from loud noises that can disturb animals.
  • Do not litter in wildlife parks.
  • Carry water but avoid plastic bottles.
  • Wear neutral-colored clothing for safaris.
  • Avoid feeding animals in the park.
  • Use binoculars for a closer view instead of approaching.

Dining Etiquette

Traditional Eating

  • Use your right hand for eating.
  • Mix rice and curry with fingers in traditional dining.
  • Wash hands thoroughly before and after meals.
  • Accept food with your right hand.
  • Do not overfill your plate; take only what you can eat.
  • Refrain from using cutlery in traditional settings unless offered.
  • Observe how locals eat and follow their lead.
  • Thank the host or server for the meal.

Dining Customs

  • Wait for elders to start eating.
  • Share food when offered.
  • Do not refuse food unless absolutely necessary.
  • Eat quietly and avoid making loud noises.
  • Compliment the host on the meal.
  • Offer to help with serving or cleaning up.
  • Respect local traditions and preferences.
  • Tip servers if dining at a restaurant.

Local Customs

Greetings

  • Use "Ayubowan" with palms together at chest level to greet respectfully.
  • Avoid handshakes unless initiated by the other person.
  • Smile and make eye contact when greeting.
  • Learn basic Sinhala or Tamil greetings for better interaction.
  • Do not hug or kiss unless you know the person well.
  • Show deference to elders when greeting.
  • Use titles like "Mr." or "Ms." unless invited to use first names.
  • Respect traditional greetings during cultural events.

Public Behavior

  • Public displays of affection are discouraged.
  • Dress modestly in public places, especially in rural areas.
  • Avoid loud or disruptive behavior in public settings.
  • Respect local laws and traditions.
  • Refrain from smoking in public places unless permitted.
  • Dispose of trash responsibly.
  • Ask permission before taking photos of people.
  • Do not point at people or objects with your finger.

Visa and Travel Tips

Visa Requirements

  • Obtain an Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) before arriving.
  • Fees vary by country; check online for the latest updates.
  • Visa extensions can be arranged at the Immigration Office in Colombo.
  • Carry printed copies of your ETA approval for easy reference.
  • Ensure your passport is valid for at least six months.
  • Declare foreign currency exceeding USD 15,000 upon arrival.
  • Check for COVID-19 travel requirements or restrictions.
  • Familiarize yourself with Sri Lanka's customs regulations.

Budget Travel Tips

  • Use public transport like buses and trains to save money.
  • Eat at local restaurants for authentic and affordable meals.
  • Book accommodations in advance for better deals.
  • Travel during the off-peak season for lower prices.
  • Use ride-sharing apps for affordable local travel.
  • Shop at local markets for souvenirs and essentials.
  • Carry a reusable water bottle to reduce costs.
  • Look for free or low-cost attractions like public beaches and parks.
